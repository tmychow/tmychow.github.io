Hey there! I'm Trevor - I'm a first year economist and Jardine Scholar at Trinity College, University of Cambridge who is from Hong Kong. I am also an Emergent Ventures grantee as awarded by the Mercatus Center. Previously I was a King's Scholar at Eton College. This website is a collection of my ramblings on economics, with a focus on business cycle macroeconomics, monetary economics and progress studies. I post around once a fortnight and you can keep up with my posts by subscribing to my monthly newsletter.

My full blog archive is available here, but some of my posts you may want to check out include:

  • A three-part primer to economic growth theory, with a paper replication in R
  • A hypothesis on what makes my hometown of Hong Kong special in its life expectancy
  • A take on why short-run fluctuations matter more than Bob Lucas might have you think
  • A review of the economic literature on open borders and migration
  • A piece explaining intermediate business cycle macroeconomics and taking the IS-LM model to its limits

I'm always interested in opportunities to get involved with economic research, policy thinktanks, and policymaking - my resume is readily available here, so feel free to get in touch. Beyond that, I love hearing what people think about my blog and connecting with those who have overlapping interests - as such, don't hesitate to message me on twitter or to contact me by email!
