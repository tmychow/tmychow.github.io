Who am I?

My Personal Background

Hey there! I'm Trevor Chow and I am an 18-year old from Hong Kong who studies in the UK. I am primarily interested in monetary policy, housing policy and computational statistics. This website is a collection of my ramblings on economics and public policy. I post around once a fortnight - feel free to message me on twitter if you have any thoughts. I also co-run an instagram and a youtube account, both of which bring attention to important social issues across the globe.

Some of my current content includes discussing economics, coronavirus, and other miscellaneous topics relating to politics, law and social movements. This content is broadly arranged into three types - shorter back-of-the-envelope takes, longer researched effortposts and interviews.

Where do I stand?

My policy positions can be summarised as "neoliberal" in a very online, reddit-centric sense. In short, I believe in evidence-based pragmatism and a general deference towards individual liberty and personal choice. A longer list of where I stand on specific issues and why I believe those things will soon be available on the Useful Files page.
